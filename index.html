Trivia + Facts

Real Easter facts, minus the boring lecture voice.

These are kid-friendly summaries based on established reference and educational sources listed at the bottom of the page.

What Easter celebratesEaster is the holiest day in Christianity. It celebrates the resurrection of Jesus, which means rising from the dead.
Why eggs are part of EasterEggs became symbols of new life and, in Christian tradition, of the Resurrection. Decorating eggs has been recorded for centuries.
Where the Easter Bunny comes fromThe rabbit became part of Easter traditions in Protestant parts of Europe in the 1600s. In the United States, the tradition is often linked to German immigrants in the 1700s.
Why the date movesEaster does not stay on the same calendar day each year. It is a movable feast, which is why it can fall on different Sundays in March or April.
Colorful eggs around the worldDifferent countries celebrate with different egg customs. In some traditions, eggs are dyed red, while in others they are painted, hidden, rolled, or given as gifts.
The White House Easter Egg RollIn the United States, the White House Easter Egg Roll has been held, with interruptions, since 1878.
Spring symbolsBunnies, chicks, flowers, and eggs are all tied to spring and new life, which is why they show up all over Easter celebrations.
Easter MondayIn some countries and communities, the day after Easter is also a holiday called Easter Monday.
